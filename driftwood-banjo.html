Fundy Driftwood

← Back to Models

A tackhead open-back banjo built with the same care as a modern instrument — traditional in form, not in feel.

The Driftwood uses a natural skin head tacked directly to the rim, eliminating modern tension hardware entirely. The result is a warmer, lighter instrument with a direct, dry tone that sits well in old-time ensembles.

Tackhead banjos often prioritize historical form over playability. The Driftwood is built to the same standards of neck geometry, relief, and setup as any other Fundy instrument. The nut and bridge are carefully fitted as part of the final setup. The natural skin head is pre-stretched and mounted carefully to help maintain stable tension. The goal is a traditional instrument that feels familiar in the hands but requires no compromise in playability.

Available fretted or fretless. The simplified construction keeps the instrument light, responsive, and easy to live with.

  • Type Tackhead open-back banjo
  • Head Natural skin, tacked to rim
  • Hardware Reduced / simplified
  • Tone Warm, dry, direct
  • Fretboard Fretted or fretless
  • Tuners Wooden friction pegs or geared tuners
  • Build Small-batch model
  • Setup Full playability setup included
